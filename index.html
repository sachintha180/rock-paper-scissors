Backstory

Pol, Kola and Lanu are Sinhala (the main language spoken in Sri Lanka) words, spelt using English characters:

  • Pol (coconut) is equivalent to Rock.
  • Kola (leaves) is equivalent to Paper.
  • Lanu (rope) is equivalent to Scissors.

This choice of these words was mainly influenced by Sri Lanka being a largely cocunut-centered economy.

The coconut tree is used for roofing (by tatching its leaves), making ropes (by weaving coconut fibres) and so much more.

We use the coconut for a lot of things, and now as a replacement to rock paper scissors too.
